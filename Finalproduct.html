About Me

Being an open book

My childhood

Allow me to reintroduce myself: I am Rose-Elizabeth Chika Karlay, my mother's second daughter and my father's fourth child. I spent my childhood in Liberia, where I was resided by my father and stepmother, Blatu, and numerous cousins until I was seven years old. Contrary to the stereotype, Blatu was far from the wicked stepmother depicted in Cinderella. She was a mother and a friend to me. Eventually, I began spending more time at my biological mother's home, where I discovered I had two additional sisters: my older sister, Victoria, and my younger sister, Gloria.

My Adolescence

At age 11, in 2017, my mother got a scholarship to study in Germany and took my little sister and me along. During the Lockdown in 2020, my mother believed it would be best for us to stay in Germany, even after her graduation. Knowing that decision, I studied hard to be fluent in the language, both in writing and speaking, made lifelong friends, and finished my Realschulabschluss with a 1.8-grade point average. As of today, I am pursuing my Fachabitur in computer science, nearing the completion of my first year of the two-year program.
